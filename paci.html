PACI sticks have replaced lockpicks.

Officially designated Polysystemic Access and Control Interface, PACI sticks are a definitive tool in the arsenal of any hacker, whether that's an engineer, thief, or operative. These five devices, now available widely but often not legally, share the ability to interface with any computer architecture in existence - with each one being designed to target one specific type.

Given that back-end access to UNIT is access to nearly anything with the right skillset and tools, lockpicks have been effectively phased out by PACI sticks in all but the most backwater of regions. Often, operatives are trained to rely on their PACI sticks instead of their gun as a last resort weapon due to their incredible potential.

Behind the reality we can see with our eyes, there is one we don't. Everything, from the most inconsequential microchip to the most powerful supercomputer, is interconnected. It's what computer scientists call the Ubiquitous-Node Internet of Things, or UNIT.

Every member of our society is expected to understand and interact with UNIT. The depth of your understanding and expertise, however, can dictate who you are in this world. Even the most rugged of operatives have to fully understand UNIT, because if they don't, it's only a matter of time before they get blindsided.
